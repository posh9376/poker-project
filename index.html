About

Poker is a popular card game that combines skill, strategy, and chance. Players bet on the value of their hands, with the goal of winning chips or money from other participants by either holding the best hand or convincing others to fold through strategic bluffing. Poker comes in various forms, like Texas Hold'em , Omaha, and Seven-Card Stud , each with its own rules and nuances. It is played in casinos, at home, and online, attracting both casual players and professional competitors in high-stakes tournaments. Success in poker relies on understanding probability, psychology, and timing.

INSRUCTIONS

  • The dealer deals two carsd to each player.
  • Post the blinds: Players place forced bets called the small blind and big blind to start of the game.
  • First betting round:Players can:
    1. call- match the existing bet (press call buton)
    2. raise - raise the current betting value
    3. fold - discard their hand and the oponent wins
  • The Flop: The dealer reveals three community cards face up on the table.
  • Second betting round: Another round of betting starts with the player left of the dealer.
  • The Turn: The dealer adds a fourth community card.
  • Third betting round: Players bet again, starting from the left of the dealer.
  • The River: A final, fifth community card is revealed.
  • Final betting round: One last chance to bet, raise, or fold.
  • Showdown: If multiple players remain, they reveal their hands. The player with the best combination of their two cards and the five community cards wins.
